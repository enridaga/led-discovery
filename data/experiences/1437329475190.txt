<pre>At Bangor, she heard the Welsh harp for the first time. The old blind harper of <br />the house was in the kitchen ; thither she went, and seemed delighted with the <br />wild and plaintive music which he played. But when he struck up a Welsh jig, she 
started up before all the servants in the kitchen, and danced as if she were <br />wild. I thought she never would have ceased. At length, however, she 
finished ; and, on quitting the kitchen, gave the harper two guineas. 
</pre>
