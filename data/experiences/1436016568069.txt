<p>It was in a saloon that Leon Rappolo first picked up a clarinet. Leon's father owned a negro saloon, and every now and then a coloured band would drop in to play a chorus as ballyhoo for a colored dance coming up or a prize fight. Late at night they'd serenade the saloon for free drinks. [...] Rappolo's kid would tease the clarinet players in these bands to teach him some licks.&nbsp;<em>And they did</em>.</p>
