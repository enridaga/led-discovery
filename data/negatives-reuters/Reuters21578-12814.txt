Trans World Airways said load factor
in March rose to 67.4 pct from 60.4 pct last year.
    Available seat miles rose 22 pct to 3.9 billion from 3.2
billion and revenue passenger miles rose 35.4 pct to 2.6
billion from 2.0 billion.
    For the year-to-date, load factor rose to 60.3 pct from
55.2 pct, available seat miles rose 10.2 pct to 11.2 billion
from 10.2 billion and revenue passenger miles rose 20.2 pct to
6.7 billion from 5.6 billion.
