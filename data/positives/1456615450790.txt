<p><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-GB;">My general impressions of the premi&egrave;re were good. Both the music and the stagecraft in the production were of a high quality. I particularly liked the conductor, Edward Downes, who is undoubtedly a splendid musician. Downes, by the way, translated the libretto into English, and though it is hard to me to judge, it seems to have been done well. A special word of praise is due for Marie Colliere, who was in the leading role. She is a marvellous actress, with a beautiful voice, and managed to convey the tragedy of Katerina&rsquo;s predicament with great skill. / The work was done conscientiously, and it turned out to be a good, integrated production.
