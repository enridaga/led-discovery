<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The vocal musicians, or singers, who have a great affinity with the <em><span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">bardi</span></em> and <em><span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">scaldi </span></em>among the ancient Germans, generally hold their meetings on festivals, and perform even in private houses for money. Music flourishes greatly at Nurenberg [<em>sic</em>], where they have musical meetings, or concerts, which they call <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>krantzel</em>.</span> Fischer on the violin, and Tenner on the German flute, are no mean performers.</span></p>
