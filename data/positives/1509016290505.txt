<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Another Evening, as we were walking on the <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Pincian Mount</em>,</span> we met with a very agreeable Entertainment, a sort of <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Carmen Am&oelig;b&oelig;um</em>,</span> much in the Manner of the old Eclogue. Two Persons had plac'd themselves under the Wall of the Duke of <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Tuscany</em>'s</span> Palace, <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Villa de' Medici</em>,</span> with their Guitars, and sang alternate [&hellip;] It was very pleasant and entertaining. Sometimes we have seen the better sort take the Fresco of the Evening in their Chaises, which in agreeable places they cause to stop, and sit in them there, singing and playing on Lutes or Guitars.</span></p>
