<pre>Mr. Logier, the inventor of a method of teaching music, to which he has given his <br />name, was living in the barracks at Limerick at that time. He belonged to the <br />Marquis of Ormonde's regiment of militia, which was quartered there. The finest 
trumpet player I ever heard in any country played in our orchestra; his execution <br />on the instrument almost baffled belief; his name was Willman, and he is the <br />brother of Mr. Willman, the principal clarionet, and an equally talented perfomer <br />on that instrument, at the King's Theatre. </pre>
