<p><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: 'Times New Roman'; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA;">Meanwhile [it was] decided that once I had passed my School Certificate I should be allowed a certain amount of time off for music during school hours. This was unprecedented, and the favouritism was resented by one or two of my class-mates, who considered any music short of Henry Hall or Harry Roy, the &lsquo;pop&rsquo; band-leaders of the day, as a mad aberration not to be encouraged.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>In retaliation I began proselytising and won quite a few converts to the serious cause.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>If I bullied, dragging my heretic friends to the nearest piano and making them listen to Bach and Beethoven, I excuse my presumption in retrospect on the grounds that a successful musician must needs be both propagandist and defence counsel for the composers he loves.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></span></p>
