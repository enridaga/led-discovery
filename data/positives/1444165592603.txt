<p>I spent one long,&nbsp;never-to-be-forgotten evening with him [Herbeck] in his mansion near the sky, talking over old times and friends, playing&nbsp;with him four-handed arrangements of his later concerted works, which I had not heard rendered by the&nbsp;orchestra, and listening to all-but-impossible feats on the&nbsp;pianoforte by Epstein and Joseffy, whom he had invited&nbsp;to meet me.</p>
