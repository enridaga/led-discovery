<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">I will now take occasion (before I proceed to the rest of the Palaces) to mention another <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Functione</em>,</span> the <em><span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;">Corpus-Christi</span></em> Procession, which is annually made from the Palace of the <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Vatican</em>;</span> they reckon it the greatest Procession they have, and call it <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Processio Generalissima</em>.</span></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">&nbsp;</span><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The Procession began with Charity-Boys, Orphans, singing Anthems in parts.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;</span></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Then follow'd the several Religious Orders (some of them singing) a prodigious Number.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">&nbsp;</span><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">After these, a Company carrying white Wax Flambeaux, some of them were Religious, some Citizens, among them Gentlemens or rich Citizens Sons, pretty Youths. The Number of the Company which bore the Flambeaux, as I was told, was about two thousand. [&hellip;] I thought there was little need of so many Flambeaux in such bright and hot Sunshine, as we then had.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">&nbsp;</span><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">Then came the Pavilions (a sort of Tents) to represent the several <span style="mso-bidi-font-style: italic;"><em>Basiliche</em>,</span> with Inscriptions upon each, and little Bells about some of them, with Choristers singing in parts, attended with several Officers, and Gentlemen with Flambeaux like-wise.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">&nbsp;</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial;">Then follow'd the Generals of the several Orders, and their Servants. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial;">Then the <em>Sbirri</em></span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial;">, with their <em>Barigello</em>, or Captain. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial;">The <em>Protonotarii Apostolici</em>. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial;">Some Officers bearing the four <em>Treregni</em> [Triple Crowns] richly adorn'd with Jewels; and three Mitres. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial;">The Musicians of the Pope's Chapel, the Prelates, the Penitentiaries of St. Peter's.</span></p>
