<p>There was but one man in Vienna &mdash; oddly&nbsp;enough, his dearest friend and heartiest admirer, Pepi&nbsp;Hellmesberger &mdash; who could cap him [Herbeck], play what he would;&nbsp;and nothing could be more interesting or amusing than&nbsp;to get these two great musical chiefs together in a mood&nbsp;to compare their classical and operatic reminiscences and&nbsp;test one another's memories anent such pleasing trivialities as Volkslieder, Laendler and Waelzer by the innumerable melodists, unknown to fame, with whom the Puchies of Austria proper have abounded for the last sixty or seventy years.&nbsp;</p>
