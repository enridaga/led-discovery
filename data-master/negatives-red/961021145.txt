"The sergeant of the guard one day asked me to lend him a book to read. I said I was afraid I'd nothing he'd care for, but I'd look. This was my Detention Cell Library: <i>Fellowship Hymn Book</i> and Weymouth; Rauschenbusch <i>Christianity and the Social Crisis</i>; <i>The Meaning of Prayer, The Manhood of the Master, and Prayers for Students</i> (S.C.M.); Otto's and Hugo's German grammars; Luther's Testament, and Goethe's Faust!'"
