<p>One of the most powerful musical recollections I have of my childhood is of my first visit to the Mariinsky Theatre, where I heard&nbsp;<em>Eugene Onegin</em>. I already knew the music, since it was often sung and played on the piano at home. But when I heard the opera played by an orchestra for the first time, I was astounded. A new world of orchestral sounds and varied instrumental colours opened up before me...</p>
