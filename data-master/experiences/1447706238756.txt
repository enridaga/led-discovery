<p>But&nbsp;&mdash;next to the inimitable Mendelssohn&mdash; of all the pianists&nbsp;I listened to with more or less rapture and envy before&nbsp;completing my tenth year, Stemdale Bennett was the&nbsp;one who most thoroughly fulfilled my boyish ideal of&nbsp;what playing ought to be. For finish, delicacy of expression, accuracy of phrasing, and a certain gentle&nbsp;grace of style that imparted an indefinable charm to his&nbsp;interpretations of classical works, I have never listened&nbsp;to anybody quite his equal.</p>
